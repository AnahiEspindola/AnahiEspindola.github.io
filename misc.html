International Panels

In 2014, I was nominated and elected to become an expert for the Intergovernmental Platform for Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES), managed by the United Nations. Since then, along with a group of worldwide experts, we have been working on writing the first IPBES assessment, which treats on Pollination, pollinators and pollination services. This has been an incredibly exciting and professionally enriching work, and I am looking forward to the publication of our first report. Here you can read a Nature article about what IPBES is and what this assessment will consist of.
Since 2015, I am an expert in pollination, pollinators and food production within the UNDP-managed Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services Network (BES-Net). The Network represents a centralized way to “promote dialogue around the same thematic areas in which IPBES assessments are being conducted, as well as in key areas of UNDP’s work on biodiversity and ecosystems management for development and provide interactive features for the members of the network to interact on specific policy questions”.

Women and Post-docs in Science

In 2015, I could take part in a Women in Math and Science event organized by the College of Sciences of the University of Idaho. Here, young women from high and middle schools are exposed to different scientific experiences and also are given a framework to discuss and meditate about the low proportion of women in STEM specialties, and to think about ways to increase and improve women representation in these areas.

Since 2014, I actively participated in the creation of a post-doc group at the University of Idaho. This group seeks to help connect post-docs, improve post-doc careers, help integrate them in the University community, as well as help resolve problems related to the fact that many people do not really know what a post-doc is (!!!).
Today, the group counts around 50 members from different Departments, and we are working on getting it recognized it with the legal status of an official Post-doc Association. Some of the activities we have worked on consisted in organizing job search workshops, establishing a mentoring program for grad students, as well as organizing monthly meetings in which post-docs can get to know each other and exchange experiences.
